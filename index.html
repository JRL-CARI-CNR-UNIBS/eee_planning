Graphical abstract of the proposed Exploration-Exploitation-Estimation approach for temporal-optimal task planning in multi-robot context: given prior distributions of actions durations, a problem instance is sampled and solved using an off-the-shelf temporal planner. During plan executions, the prior distributions are updated thanks to the Kalman Filters. Posterior distributions then used to sample new problem instances in subsequent goal requests, balancing exploration and exploitation.

Abstract

Time efficiency is paramount in robotic applications, as it significantly impacts competitiveness in industrial environments and user satisfaction in social contexts. Off-the-shelf temporal planners commonly operate under the assumption that the durations of actions are precisely known. However, this assumption often fails in dynamic environments, when robots share the workspace with humans, and uncertainty in action durations can significantly impact operational efficiency. In this paper, we address the challenge of temporal planning in dynamic settings where action durations are unknown or uncertain by using a Kalman filter that updates action duration distributions across executions. These updated duration distributions are then leveraged to generate new problem instances within a posterior sampling architecture, which manages the exploration-exploitation trade-off. The proposed method was tested in a simulated social scenario with two mobile robots executing service tasks. Experimental results demonstrate that our method reduces mismatches between estimated and actual execution times, adapts to the actual durations, achieves faster task completion, and reduces the risk of getting stuck in suboptimal solutions. Additionally, the proposed method was validated through an industrial case study in intralogistics, highlighting its relevance in real-world multi-robot settings.

The project follows a modular architecture and is implemented across multiple repositories, each addressing a specific component of the proposed methodology. This section lists and describes all repositories associated with the project. The source code will be made publicly available after the paper has completed the peer-review process.

Architecture of the proposed Exploration-Exploitation-Estimation approach integrated with PlanSys2. Go to Related repositories to see the github repos of each involved module.

Integration of the nominal action cost computation with the auction mechanism of PlanSys2. The auction mechanism is modified to support the integration with the proposed method. The action cost is computed using the path duration cost given the path obtained throug interaction with nav2 actions.

Integration of the proposed method with off-the-shelf temporal planners. They have to support fluents in durations of durative actions. These fluents will be associated to the Kalman Filter.
